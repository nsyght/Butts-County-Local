Moving to Jackson, GA? Your Complete New Resident Guide

March 3, 2026  ·  9 min read
← Back to Blog

So you're moving to Jackson, GA — or at least considering it. Welcome to one of Middle Georgia's most underrated communities. Here's what you need to know before you arrive, and where to find everything once you're here.

The Big Picture

Jackson is the county seat of Butts County, located about 50 miles south of Atlanta along I-75. The population hovers around 5,000 in the city, with about 25,000 in the county overall. It's genuinely small-town Georgia — in all the best ways. The pace is slower, the neighbors know each other, and the land is beautiful.

Cost of Living

One of the primary draws for people relocating from Atlanta and other metros is the dramatically lower cost of living. Housing prices in Butts County remain well below the state average, and you'll find everything from historic homes on the courthouse square to new construction subdivisions on the county's outskirts.

Schools

The Butts County School System serves K-12 students across five schools. The district has been investing in facilities and programs in recent years. Jackson High School is home to the Red Devils, and athletics are a significant part of community life. Check the district's website at bcssk12.org for enrollment information.

Healthcare

For primary care, Butts County has several local clinics including Aylo Health on W. 3rd Street, which accepts walk-in appointments and is open evenings and weekends. For dental care, Bhat Dental Associates is highly rated with nearly 400 Google reviews. The nearest major hospital facilities are in McDonough and Griffin.

Getting Around

Jackson is a car-dependent community — there's no public transit to speak of. I-75 provides quick access north to Atlanta (about an hour in normal traffic) and south toward Macon. GA-16 connects east toward Monticello and west toward Griffin.

Finding Your Community

The best way to plug in quickly is through local events, the Butts County Chamber of Commerce, and by patronizing downtown businesses. The courthouse square is the social hub of the community, and local restaurants like Lucy Lu's Coffee and Mesquite Mexican Grill are natural gathering spots.
