New to Jackson, GA?

Welcome to Butts County. Here's everything you need to get settled in your new community.

🏘️ About Jackson & Butts County

Jackson is the county seat of Butts County, located about 50 miles south of Atlanta along I-75. Population: ~5,000 in the city, ~25,000 in the county. It's genuine small-town Georgia — slower pace, friendly neighbors, beautiful land, and a strong sense of community. Butts County was founded in 1825 and is one of Georgia's most historically rich counties.

Fun fact: Downtown Jackson was used as the filming location for Hawkins, Indiana in Netflix's Stranger Things. The Butts County Courthouse appears in the show as the Hawkins Public Library.

🏫 Schools

The Butts County School System serves K-12 students across five schools including Jackson High School (home of the Red Devils). The district has been investing in facilities and expanding programs in recent years.

🏥 Healthcare

Primary care is available locally at Aylo Health on W. 3rd Street (walk-ins welcome, extended hours). Bhat Dental Associates is the county's top-rated dental practice. For major hospital services, Piedmont Henry Hospital in McDonough and Piedmont Spalding in Griffin are the nearest facilities.

🍽️ Local Favorites

Start with these local institutions: Fresh Air Barbecue (a Georgia legend since 1929), Lucy Lu's Coffee Cafe (downtown's gathering spot), Mesquite Mexican Grill (best tacos in town), and Hunter's Cafe for classic Southern breakfast.

🌲 Parks & Outdoors

You're surrounded by some of Georgia's finest outdoor destinations. Indian Springs State Park (one of the oldest in the nation) and High Falls State Park are minutes away. Dauset Trails Nature Center offers free admission and 17 miles of trails with native wildlife.

🏛️ Government & Services

Key local government contacts for new residents. Vehicle registration and property tax handled through Butts County Tax Commissioner. Georgia driver's license through the DDS office in Jackson.

📚 Library & Community

The Jackson-Butts County Library on E. College Street is a wonderful community resource with books, digital resources, free Wi-Fi, and regular programs. The Butts County Chamber of Commerce is a great way to plug into the local business community.
